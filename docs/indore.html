City Yatra – Indore

Indore, the commercial capital of Madhya Pradesh, is a vibrant blend of tradition and modernity. From Rajwada’s royal charm to Sarafa Bazaar’s midnight food trail, Indore is a city that never sleeps — and always feeds you well!

About Indore

  • Founded by: Holkar Dynasty (18th century)
  • Population: ~2.5 million
  • Elevation: 553 m
  • Official Language: Hindi
  • Nickname: Mini Mumbai
  • Famous for: Cleanliness, food, textile industry, and education

Top Attractions

  • Rajwada Palace: Iconic 7-storey royal residence
  • Khajrana Ganesh Temple: Sacred and serene
  • Central Museum: Ancient artifacts and sculptures
  • Patalpani Waterfall: Scenic monsoon getaway
  • Chappan Dukan: 56 legendary food stalls

Local Food Highlights

  • Indori Poha: Light, fluffy, and topped with sev
  • Sabudana Khichdi: Fasting favorite
  • Garadu: Spicy fried root veggie (winter special)
  • Jaleba: Giant jalebi with a twist
  • Sarafa Bazaar: Midnight street food carnival

Travel Tips

  • Best Time: October to February
  • Transport: Buses, autos, and BRTS corridor
  • Nearby: Ujjain, Mandu, Maheshwar
